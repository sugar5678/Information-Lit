Knowledge Based Approach-
aims at using sources external to the report to verify it before it spreads , such as having it be analyzed by professionals, or by administering users with an automated fact checking procedure.
News Parody vs News Satire vs News Fabrication
An example of news satire would be something like The Daily Show where they give people news updates but often with exaggeration and a comedic twist. News parodies are aimed at presenting entirely made up news stories with a comedic twist, such as the Onion. News fabrication refers to articles which are made to appear like factual information however the authors have a deliberate intent of misinforming their audiences.

Language Method-
Use of linguistics by a software or a human to detect fake news- analysis of grammar, syntax, writing style, considering of all of the letters in a word and all the words in a sentence are used.

Machine Learning
-Data Sets are used to train algorithms to determine misinformation, an example would be the Twitter Crawler, which gathers and collects tweets and makes comparisons between them.

Determining reliable sources:
Ways for everyday people to determine reliable sources include checking for things such as .org or .gov at the end of the site name and making sure the source is current.

Top Agnostic Based Approach-
Some might think that there is no such thing as idenfiying minsinformation online without considered the content of the information. The top agnositc based approach is a non-content based way of determining misinformation. It uses web markup and linguistic features to try to determine whether or not something is fake news.Examples of this method of analysis mainly revolve around checking to see if the article has a large amount of advertisements, different patterns of text from mainstream news which seem to cause emotional responses, and whether or not the article has a name for the author.
